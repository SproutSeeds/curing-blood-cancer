## Purpose

This map summarizes what the public repo is asking experts to validate, then
names public-facing specialists and organizations who appear well matched to
those validation questions.

It is an outreach planning artifact. It does not imply that any named person or
organization has reviewed, endorsed, agreed to participate in, or validated
this project.

Use public institutional contact routes and the linked GitHub issues. Do not
send patient-identifying data, private records, portal exports, credentials,
or patient-specific clinical questions.

## What Needs Expert Validation

The current public review surface contains 13 expert-validation issues across
two packets:

- [BCMA Claim Set Expert Review Packet v0](bcma-claim-set-expert-review-packet-v0.md)
- [Multidisciplinary Review Packet Template v0](multidisciplinary-review-packet-template-v0.md)
- [Expert Validation Issue Index v0](expert-validation-issue-index-v0.md)

The issues ask experts to pressure-test public language, provenance, assay and
measurement context, source maturity, translational boundaries, safety framing,
trial-registry boundaries, access-language boundaries, and patient-facing
readability.

They do not ask experts to choose treatment, rank options, interpret a case,
match a trial, guide expanded access, or declare a cure.

Public response intake and allowed dispositions are tracked in
[Expert Response Validation Ledger v0](expert-response-validation-ledger-v0.md).
Use that ledger for status changes; do not copy private correspondence into
public artifacts.

## Standard Outreach Ask

Use a narrow ask:

> We are building a public, research-use-only multiple myeloma artifact set
> stewarded by frg.earth. We are not asking for medical advice, treatment
> guidance, trial guidance, or endorsement. We are asking whether the linked
> public language is accurate, sufficiently modest, source-backed, and safe
> from accidental clinical interpretation. If you are willing, could you review
> the specific GitHub issue that matches your expertise and either suggest
> weaker wording, identify missing context, or confirm that the current
> boundary is appropriate for public research infrastructure?

Each outreach should link one issue, not the whole repo, unless the reviewer
asks for broader context.

## What A Good Expert Response Should Include

- what wording should change, if any
- what source or measurement context is missing, if any
- whether the current claim level should remain `expert-review-needed`
- what stronger claim remains blocked
- what readers must not infer from the review
- whether any next step requires private data, clinical judgment, trial-site
  verification, sponsor input, institutional review, FDA/regulatory process, or
  other non-public action

## Public Safety Check

- No patient-identifying data.
- No real case data.
- No private records.
- No credentials, paid APIs, account changes, or restricted datasets.
- No patient-specific diagnosis, prognosis, treatment, trial, expanded-access,
  monitoring, or candidate-option guidance.
- No target, therapy, trial, source, mechanism, artifact, task, or evidence
  ranking.
- No generated biomedical claims.
- No expert-review substitution before qualified reviewers respond.
- No publication authorization.
- No claim that multiple myeloma has been cured.
- No vaccine claim.

## Limitations

- This is a public outreach shortlist, not a completed review.
- Named people and organizations have not endorsed, reviewed, or joined this
  project unless they say so in a public issue or follow-up artifact.
- The list is based on public profiles, publications, and institutional pages
  available during the review date.
- Roles and affiliations can change; verify current details before outreach.
- The list is not comprehensive.
- The list does not rank scientific authority or evidence quality.
- Issue responses must be reviewed before artifact status changes.
- Expert-review-needed status remains until a follow-up artifact records the
  review outcome.
- This map does not inspect private lab records or real case data.
- This map does not authorize generated claims, ranking, recommendation
  behavior, patient matching, trial matching, review-packet assembly, or
  publication workflow.
- This map does not provide medical advice, diagnostic guidance, treatment
  guidance, trial guidance, eligibility guidance, expanded-access guidance,
  monitoring guidance, or a cure claim.
